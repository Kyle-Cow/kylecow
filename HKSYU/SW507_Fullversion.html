為何聚焦CSSA而非其他政策？ Why Focus on CSSA and Not Other Policies?

在眾多扶貧政策中，我們選擇聚焦CSSA，因為它是香港社會福利體系的基石與最後安全網，最能深刻反映制度的內在價值與結構性矛盾。 Among the many anti-poverty policies, we choose to focus on CSSA because it is the cornerstone and final safety net of Hong Kong's social welfare system, best reflecting the system's intrinsic values and structural contradictions.

  • 核心性：CSSA直接處理「最弱勢家庭」的生存風險，是最低生活水平的保障。 Core Role: CSSA directly addresses the survival risks of the "most vulnerable families" and serves as a guarantee for the minimum standard of living.
  • 代表性：相對於在職家庭津貼等與就業掛鉤的政策，CSSA更能反映社會對公民基本權利的「去商品化」底線。 Representativeness: Compared to policies linked to employment like the Working Family Allowance, CSSA better reflects society's bottom line on the "de-commodification" of basic citizen rights.
  • 系統性：以CSSA為主軸，能有效串連起在職貧窮、住房負擔、無酬照顧責任及社會污名等相互交織的宏觀議題。 Systemic Relevance: Using CSSA as the main axis allows for an effective connection between intertwined macro issues such as in-work poverty, housing burden, unpaid care responsibilities, and social stigma.

各位教授、同學早安。今天我們將深入探討香港的低收入家庭支援政策。在開始前，首先要解答一個關鍵問題：為何在眾多政策中，我們選擇聚焦於綜援(CSSA)？ Good morning, professors and classmates. Today, we will delve into the support policies for low-income families in Hong Kong. Before we begin, we must first answer a key question: Why, among the many policies, do we choose to focus on the Comprehensive Social Security Assistance (CSSA)?

Lastly, CSSA acts like a prism; through it, we can clearly see how various macro issues like in-work poverty, the housing crisis, care responsibilities, and social stigma intertwine to push families into hardship.

因此，剖析CSSA，就是剖析香港扶貧政策的根源與靈魂。接下來，我們將首先檢視其制度脈絡。 Therefore, to analyze CSSA is to analyze the root and soul of Hong Kong's anti-poverty policy. Next, we will first examine its institutional context.

制度功能與意識形態：權利還是恩恤？ Institutional Function & Ideology: Right or Charity?

核心功能 Core Functions

  • 補足家庭收入至政府訂定的最低生活線。 Supplementing family income to meet the government-set minimum subsistence level.
  • 提供標準金額（基本生活費）、補助金及特別津貼（如租金、學費等）。 Providing standard rates (for basic living expenses), supplements, and special grants (e.g., for rent, school fees).

制度定位：殘補式福利 (Residual Welfare) Institutional Positioning: Residual Welfare

  • 政府只在市場與家庭支援失效時才介入，作為「保底」角色。 The government intervenes only when the market and family support fail, acting as a "safety net of last resort."
  • 設有嚴格的入息及資產審查（Means-testing），以控制公共開支及防止「濫用」。 It involves strict means-testing to control public expenditure and prevent "abuse."

意識形態辯證：公民權利 vs. 酌情恩恤 Ideological Dialectic: A Citizen's Right vs. Discretionary Charity

CSSA的設計充滿矛盾：它一方面承認市民有免於赤貧的基本需要，但另一方面，其嚴苛的審查、規訓式的管理和社會污名化，都使其更像一種**有條件的、由上而下的恩恤(Charity)**，而非一項**公民應有的權利(Right)**。這種意識形態上的模糊，是理解其一切問題的根源。 The design of CSSA is full of contradictions: on one hand, it acknowledges the basic need of citizens to be free from destitution. On the other hand, its harsh vetting, disciplinary management, and social stigmatization make it more like a **conditional, top-down form of charity** rather than an **inalienable citizen's right**. This ideological ambiguity is the root of all its problems.

CSSA的核心功能很明確：補足收入至最低水平。它的定位是「殘補式福利」，即政府在市場和家庭都失效後的最後防線。 CSSA's core function is clear: to supplement income to a minimum level. Its positioning is that of "residual welfare," meaning it is the last line of defense after the market and family have failed.

但我們要提出的核心批判是：這種設計背後的意識形態，究竟視福利為公民的「權利」，還是一種「恩恤」？ But the core critique we want to raise is: does the ideology behind this design treat welfare as a citizen's "right" or as a form of "charity"?

This ideology, which frames welfare as "charity" rather than a "right," is the very source of its many problems, such as stigmatization and high application barriers. Understanding this is crucial for our subsequent critique.

理論剖析(一)：新自由主義的治理邏輯與盲點 Theory I: Neoliberalism's Governing Logic & Blind Spots

治理邏輯 Governing Logic

  • 意識形態：強調「小政府、大市場」，視福利為市場效率的潛在威脅。 Ideology: Emphasizes "small government, big market," viewing welfare as a potential threat to market efficiency.
  • 個人責任化：將貧窮歸因於個人選擇或努力不足，而非結構性因素。 Individualization of Responsibility: Attributes poverty to individual choices or lack of effort, rather than structural factors.
  • 績效問責：偏好量化指標（如脫離綜援人數）來衡量政策「效率」，忽略人的福祉。 Performance Accountability: Prefers quantitative indicators (e.g., the number of people leaving CSSA) to measure policy "efficiency," ignoring human well-being.

對CSSA的影響 Impact on CSSA

  • 嚴格審查與低給付：旨在維持工作倫理和市場紀律。 Strict Vetting & Low Payouts: Aims to maintain work ethics and market discipline.
  • 污名化副作用：制度設計本身就在製造「福利依賴者」與「勤奮工作者」的對立，產生社會排斥。 Side Effect of Stigmatization: The system's design inherently creates a dichotomy between "welfare dependents" and "diligent workers," leading to social exclusion.

結構性盲點 Structural Blind Spots

新自由主義的致命盲點，在於它完全無視了**前端結構性不公**對**末端福利政策**的衝擊。例如： The fatal blind spot of neoliberalism is its complete disregard for the impact of **front-end structural injustices** on **back-end welfare policies**. For example:

  • 高地價政策作為宏觀經濟選擇，其後果是CSSA的租金津貼變相成為對業主的補貼，而非對家庭的支援。 The **high land price policy**, as a macroeconomic choice, results in CSSA rent allowances effectively becoming subsidies for landlords rather than support for families.
  • 對無酬照顧勞動的漠視，使女性和單親家庭在勞動市場上處於先天不利位置，增加了他們對CSSA的需求。 The **disregard for unpaid care labor** places women and single-parent families at an inherent disadvantage in the labor market, increasing their need for CSSA.

要理解CSSA為何是今天的模樣，必須理解其背後的主導意識形態——新自由主義。它的核心邏輯是市場至上，認為貧窮是個人不努力的結果，因此政府的角色應盡可能小，避免「養懶人」。 To understand why CSSA is the way it is today, we must understand its dominant underlying ideology: neoliberalism. Its core logic is market supremacy, which posits that poverty is the result of individual lack of effort. Therefore, the government's role should be as minimal as possible to avoid "nurturing the lazy."

這種思想直接體現在CSSA的設計上：極其嚴格的審查、僅僅維持生存的低給付水平，都是為了確保工作的「吸引力」永遠高於領取援助。但這種設計的副作用，就是將申請者標籤化，製造社會對立。 This thinking is directly reflected in CSSA's design: extremely strict vetting and low payouts that barely sustain life are all intended to ensure that the "attraction" of work is always greater than receiving assistance. But the side effect of this design is the labeling of applicants, creating social division.

更重要的是，新自由主義的理論框架有巨大的盲點。它完全無法解釋香港的現實：在一個由地產霸權主導、缺乏租金管制的市場，一個低收入工人的大部分薪水都被租金吞噬，這不是個人努力可以改變的。同樣，它也忽視了大量存在的、由女性承擔的無酬照顧工作，這些工作沒有市場價值，卻是社會運作的基石。這些結構性因素，才是導致貧窮的根本原因。 More importantly, the neoliberal theoretical framework has huge blind spots. It completely fails to explain the reality of Hong Kong: in a market dominated by real estate hegemony and lacking rent control, a large portion of a low-income worker's salary is consumed by rent—a situation that individual effort cannot change. Likewise, it ignores the vast amount of unpaid care work, largely undertaken by women, which has no market value but is the bedrock of social functioning. These structural factors are the true root causes of poverty.

理論剖析(二)：福利體制與被遺漏的群體 Theory II: The Welfare Regime and the Excluded

香港的定位 Hong Kong's Positioning

  • 根據Esping-Andersen的理論，香港屬於典型的**自由主義/殘補式福利體制**。 According to Esping-Andersen's theory, Hong Kong belongs to a typical **liberal/residual welfare regime**.
  • 特點：手段審查(Means-testing)嚴格、福利的普及性低、公民脫離市場而生存的能力（即「去商品化」程度）極弱。 Characteristics: Strict means-testing, low universality of benefits, and a very weak capacity for citizens to survive outside the market (i.e., a low degree of "de-commodification").

東亞脈絡的特殊性 The Specificity of the East Asian Context

  • 混合了儒家**家庭主義**，預設家庭是福利的第一道防線，進一步減輕了國家的責任。 It incorporates Confucian **familialism**, which presumes the family as the first line of welfare, further reducing the state's responsibility.
  • 強調**工作倫理**，福利與勞動參與緊密掛鉤。 It emphasizes a **work ethic**, tightly linking welfare to labor market participation.

安全網的漏洞：被遺漏的群體 Gaps in the Safety Net: The Excluded Groups

這種體制最大的問題，是在邊界地帶製造了大量的「福利孤兒」，他們因不完全符合資格而被安全網漏掉： The biggest problem with this regime is that it creates numerous "welfare orphans" at the margins, who fall through the safety net because they do not fully meet the eligibility criteria:

  • 在職貧窮/近貧戶 (N無人士)：收入僅僅略高於綜援線，卻無法享受任何支援。 The Working Poor / Near-Poor ("N-nothing" households): Their income is just slightly above the CSSA line, yet they are ineligible for any support.
  • 非典型就業者 (零散工)：收入不穩，難以通過僵化的入息審查。 Atypical Workers (Gig workers): With unstable incomes, they struggle to pass the rigid income assessment.
  • 部分單親家庭和照顧者：因照顧責任無法滿足工時要求。 Some Single Parents and Carers: They cannot meet the work-hour requirements due to caregiving responsibilities.

從福利體制的角度看，香港被歸類為「自由主義」或「殘補式」的體制。這個理論的核心概念是「去商品化」——指的是一個公民在多大程度上可以不依賴市場（即工作收入）而維持有尊嚴的生活。 From a welfare regime perspective, Hong Kong is classified as a "liberal" or "residual" system. The core concept of this theory is "de-commodification"—which refers to the degree to which a citizen can maintain a dignified life without relying on the market (i.e., earned income).

在香港，答案顯然是：程度極低。我們的福利設計，從綜援到強積金，都假設你必須是一個勞動者才能生存。這套體制在東亞脈絡下，還加上了「家庭主義」的元素，即預設家庭會承擔大部分照顧責任，這進一步合理化了政府的低度介入。 In Hong Kong, the answer is clearly: to a very low degree. Our welfare design, from CSSA to the MPF, assumes that one must be a laborer to survive. In the East Asian context, this system is also infused with "familialism," which presumes that the family will bear most of the care responsibilities, further justifying the government's minimal intervention.

這種制度最大的問題，是它在邊界地帶製造了大量的「福利孤兒」。例如，收入僅僅略高於綜援線的N無人士、無法找到足夠工時的單親媽媽、或是從事零散工作的青年，他們往往無法得到任何制度性的支援。這給我們的啟示是，一個好的安全網，不僅要看它接住了多少人，更要看它漏掉了多少人。 The biggest problem with this system is that it creates a large number of "welfare orphans" at the margins. For example, the "N-nothing" households whose income is just slightly above the CSSA line, single mothers who cannot find enough work hours, or young people in precarious gig work often fail to receive any institutional support. This teaches us that a good safety net should not only be judged by how many people it catches, but also by how many it misses.

理論剖析(三)：社會投資視角—從補助到能力建構 Theory III: The Social Investment Perspective—From Subsidy to Capacity Building

核心理念 Core Ideas

  • 範式轉移：將社會支出視為對未來的「投資」，而非單純的「開銷」。 Paradigm Shift: Viewing social expenditure as an "investment" in the future, rather than a mere "expense."
  • 生命週期觀點：強調在人生的關鍵階段（特別是兒童早期）進行介入，以獲取長期回報。 Life-Course Perspective: Emphasizing intervention at critical life stages (especially early childhood) to yield long-term returns.

政策重點 Policy Focus

  • 幼兒早期教育與照顧 (e.g. 可負擔的托兒服務)。 Early childhood education and care (e.g., affordable childcare services).
  • 終身學習與技能再培訓。 Lifelong learning and skills retraining.
  • 促進工作與家庭平衡的政策 (e.g. 育兒假)。 Policies that promote work-family balance (e.g., parental leave).

對CSSA的啟發與限制 Implications for CSSA & Its Limitations

啟發：我們應在CSSA中大幅增加對兒童、青年和照顧者的支援元素，例如設立更高的兒童津貼、提供照顧者津貼、或將領取援助與教育培訓機會掛鉤，將短期現金補助轉化為中長期的能力提升。 Implications: We should significantly increase support elements for children, youth, and carers within CSSA. For instance, by establishing higher child allowances, providing carer allowances, or linking assistance to educational and training opportunities, we can transform short-term cash subsidies into medium-to-long-term capacity building.

限制：社會投資回報期長，需要穩定的政治意願和財政投入。同時，我們必須警惕將所有貧窮問題都簡化為個人「技能不足」的風險，而忽略了結構性改革的必要性。 Limitations: Social investment has a long return period, requiring stable political will and financial commitment. At the same time, we must be wary of the risk of oversimplifying all poverty issues into individual "skill deficiencies," thereby ignoring the need for structural reform.

社會投資理論為我們提供了跳出傳統福利思維的第三種視角。它主張，最好的扶貧政策不是在人們陷入困境後派錢，而是在他們生命的早期階段就進行投資，從根本上預防貧窮的發生。 The social investment theory offers a third perspective that breaks from traditional welfare thinking. It argues that the best anti-poverty policy is not to hand out money after people fall into hardship, but to invest in the early stages of their lives to prevent poverty from occurring in the first place.

它的政策重點非常清晰：優質的幼兒教育、可負擔的托兒服務、持續的技能培訓。這些都不是傳統意義上的「救濟」，而是對「人力資本」的投資，目標是提升整個社會的生產力和韌性。 Its policy focus is very clear: high-quality early childhood education, affordable childcare, and continuous skills training. These are not "relief" in the traditional sense, but "investments" in "human capital," aimed at enhancing the productivity and resilience of the entire society.

這對改革CSSA有重要啟發。我們可以在現有框架內，大幅增加對兒童相關開支的補助，或者為需要照顧家庭的單親家長提供津貼，讓她們有機會去進修。目標是將被動的現金援助，轉化為主動的能力建設。當然，我們也要意識到它的局限性：社會投資需要長期承擔，且不能成為政府推卸結構性改革（如解決住房問題）責任的藉口。 This has important implications for reforming CSSA. Within the existing framework, we can significantly increase subsidies for child-related expenses or provide allowances for single parents who need to care for their families, giving them opportunities for further education. The goal is to transform passive cash assistance into active capacity building. Of course, we must also be aware of its limitations: social investment requires long-term commitment and must not become an excuse for the government to evade its responsibility for structural reforms (such as solving the housing problem).

制度失靈的證據：數據與現實 Evidence of System Failure: Data and Reality

1. 住房壓力使津貼失效 1. Housing Pressure Nullifies Subsidies

根據Wong & Chan (2019)等學者的研究，高昂的租金是侵蝕CSSA津貼購買力的最主要因素。租金津貼的上限遠低於市場水平，導致許多家庭需要用本應用於食物、教育的基本生活費去補貼租金，令脫貧難上加難。 According to research by scholars like Wong & Chan (2019), exorbitant rents are the primary factor eroding the purchasing power of CSSA allowances. The cap on rent allowance is far below market levels, forcing many families to use their basic living expenses intended for food and education to cover rent, making it even harder to escape poverty.

2. 污名與程序負擔導致的「非申請型排斥」 2. "Non-Take-Up Exclusion" Caused by Stigma and Procedural Burdens

  • 程序負擔：繁複的文件要求、漫長的審批時間、侵入性的家訪，都構成巨大的「程序焦慮」。 Procedural Burdens: Complicated document requirements, lengthy approval times, and intrusive home visits all create significant "procedural anxiety."
  • 污名效應：社會標籤導致符合資格的家庭（特別是長者和在職貧窮）因害怕被歧視而選擇不申請。 Stigma Effect: Social labeling causes eligible families (especially the elderly and the working poor) to opt out of applying for fear of discrimination.

3. 就業誘因的「貧窮陷阱」 3. The "Poverty Trap" of Work Incentives

現行的收入豁免額度過低，導致受助人工作收入增加時，大部分會被扣減，即「邊際有效稅率」過高。這種「多做多扣」的體感，嚴重削弱了尋找更高收入工作的動力，使人困在低薪工作與援助之間。 The current income disregard level is too low, causing a large portion of a recipient's increased earnings to be deducted, resulting in a high "effective marginal tax rate." This feeling of "earning more means getting less" severely dampens the motivation to seek higher-paying jobs, trapping individuals between low-wage work and assistance.

4. 個案結構轉變的警號 4. A Warning Sign in Caseload Structure Changes

6. 制度失靈的證據：數據與現實 (4分鐘)

  • 住房壓力使津貼失效：租金津貼上限遠低於市場，變相補貼業主。
  • 「非申請型排斥」：污名與程序負擔導致合資格者卻步。
  • 就業誘因的「貧窮陷阱」：低收入豁免額造成「多做多扣」。
  • 個案結構轉變的警號：健全人士個案下降，可能意味在職貧窮被排斥。

7. 國際比較 (4分鐘)

  • 英國 (Universal Credit): 整合性支付系統簡化程序，但數碼鴻溝和嚴格的條件限制帶來挑戰。
  • 台灣 (低收入戶及中低收入戶補助): 「兩級制」設計有效避免「福利懸崖」，並提供整合性服務。
  • 澳門 (經濟援助): 相對慷慨的援助水平更能應對生活成本，反映政府財政決心和社會價值觀。

8. 社會工作者的建議 (5分鐘)

  • 微觀（個案／家庭）：申請支援與權益教育；連結個人化服務（托兒、精神健康、就業配對）。
  • 中觀（機構／社區）：建立「申請易」協作網絡；推動「過渡屋＋社工隊」模式。
  • 宏觀（政策倡議）：優化CSSA（提高豁免額、租津）、打通與WFA及職訓的政策銜接。

9. 反思與專業定位 (2分鐘)

  • 專業角色：制度的翻譯者、證據的倡議者、倫理的守門人。
  • 可行行動：參與數據蒐集、撰寫立場書、公共溝通去污名。
  • 核心價值：在所有層級的實踐中落實公平、尊重、關懷、誠信。

Social Welfare Support for Low-Income Families in Hong Kong

A Critical Analysis of Macro-Structural Factors in the CSSA Policy

SW507 Course Group Presentation Outline

Group Members: Li Ka Kit (牛), Lam Ka Wing (Hebe), Liu Pik Ha (Vivian), Siu King San (Kingson), Cheung Ching Long (Isaac)

Date: October 2025

1. Why Focus on CSSA? (1.5 min)

  • Core Role: HK's minimum living protection and final safety net.
  • Representativeness: Best reflects the "de-commodification" baseline and institutional fairness.
  • Systemic Relevance: Connects issues of in-work poverty, housing burden, care responsibilities, and stigma.

2. Institutional Function & Ideology: Right or Charity? (2.5 min)

  • Core Function: Supplements family income to the minimum subsistence level.
  • Institutional Positioning: Residual welfare with means-testing to control expenditure.
  • Ideological Dialectic: Designed more like a conditional "Charity" than a citizen's "Right".

3. Theory I: Neoliberalism's Governing Logic & Blind Spots (2 min)

  • Governing Logic: Small government, individual responsibility, performance accountability.
  • Impact on CSSA: Strict vetting, low payouts, side effect of stigmatization.
  • Structural Blind Spots: Disregards front-end injustices like high land prices and unpaid care labor.

4. Theory II: The Welfare Regime and the Excluded (2 min)

  • Positioning: Liberal/residual type, weak de-commodification.
  • System Gaps: Tends to miss groups like the "near-poor," atypical workers, and single carers.

5. Theory III: The Social Investment Perspective (2 min)

  • Core Idea: Shift from "expense" to "investment" in human capital.
  • Implication for CSSA: Enhance elements for children and carers to turn subsidies into capacity building.

6. Evidence of System Failure: Data and Reality (4 min)

  • Housing Pressure Nullifies Subsidies: Rent allowance lags far behind market rates.
  • "Non-Take-Up Exclusion": Stigma and procedural burdens deter eligible applicants.
  • The "Poverty Trap": Low income disregard levels disincentivize work.
  • Warning Sign in Caseload: Declining able-bodied cases may indicate exclusion of the working poor.

7. International Comparison (4 min)

  • UK (Universal Credit): Integrated system simplifies applications but poses digital exclusion risks.
  • Taiwan (Low & Mid-Low-Income Subsidies): "Two-tier" design effectively avoids the "welfare cliff."
  • Macau (Financial Assistance): More generous assistance levels reflect political will and social values.

8. Social Work Recommendations (5 min)

  • Micro (Case/Family): Application support and rights education; connecting to personalized services.
  • Meso (Organization/Community): "Easy-Apply" collaborative network; "Transitional Housing + Social Work Team" model.
  • Macro (Policy Advocacy): Optimize CSSA (raise disregards, rent allowance); create pathways between welfare and training programs.

9. Reflection & Professional Role (2 min)

  • Professional Roles: Translator of the system, Advocate with evidence, Guardian of ethics.
  • Actionable Steps: Participate in data collection, write position papers, engage in public education to de-stigmatize.
  • Core Values: Uphold social justice and respect for dignity in all levels of practice.
