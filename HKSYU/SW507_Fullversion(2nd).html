引言：CSSA 背景及重要性 Introduction: CSSA Background and Importance

CSSA 背景 CSSA Background

  • CSSA 作為殘餘式福利模式的安全網，提供基本生活援助，但忽略結構性貧窮（如高生活成本）。 CSSA as a residual welfare model safety net, providing basic living assistance but overlooking structural poverty (e.g., high living costs).
  • 政策目標：減少長期依賴，強調就業激勵，但導致批評為加劇不平等。 Policy Goals: Reduce long-term dependency, emphasize employment incentives, but criticized for exacerbating inequality.

現實挑戰 Real Challenges

  • 嚴格手段測試排除邊緣貧窮者；工作導向忽略照顧需求。 Strict means-testing excludes marginal poor; work-oriented approach ignores care needs.
  • 連結宏觀結構：高地價與市場化加劇貧窮。 Linking Macro Structures: High land prices and marketization exacerbate poverty.
📝 詳細講稿📝 Full Script▼

CSSA 是香港福利體系的核心，旨在為無法自力更生的家庭提供最後安全網。但其設計反映新自由主義，強調小政府與個人責任，導致嚴格審查與低援助水平。這忽略了結構性因素，如高生活成本與就業不穩，批評它未能實現平等。根據最新貧窮報告 (Census and Statistics Department, 2019; HKCSS 最新報告)，貧窮率持續高企，CSSA 非領取率高達30%，反映污名化與申請障礙。我們將批判性分析其權利 vs. 責任辯論，並提出可持續策略。 CSSA is the core of Hong Kong's welfare system, aiming to provide a last safety net for families unable to be self-reliant. However, its design reflects neoliberalism, emphasizing small government and individual responsibility, leading to strict vetting and low assistance levels. This overlooks structural factors like high living costs and employment instability, criticizing it for failing to achieve equality. According to the latest poverty reports (Census and Statistics Department, 2019; HKCSS latest report), poverty rates remain high, with CSSA non-take-up rates up to 30%, reflecting stigmatization and application barriers. We will critically analyze the rights vs. responsibilities debate and propose sustainable strategies.

福利理念：新自由主義核心特徵及在CSSA的體現 Welfare Ideology: Neoliberalism Core Features and Embodiment in CSSA

新自由主義核心特徵 Neoliberalism Core Features

  • 強調自由市場競爭、放鬆管制、私有化、個人自力更生 (Lecture 2, Slide 10)。 Emphasizes free market competition, deregulation, privatization, individual self-reliance (Lecture 2, Slide 10).
  • 小政府、大市場；市場機制解決社會問題 (Mudge, 2008)。 Small government, big market; market mechanisms solve social problems (Mudge, 2008).

CSSA的新自由主義體現 CSSA's Neoliberal Embodiment

  • 手段測試：嚴格經濟審查、資產限制，排除邊緣貧窮者。 Means-testing: Strict financial vetting, asset limits, excluding marginal poor.
  • 工作導向：自力更生支援計劃 (SSR)，強調就業激勵，減少依賴。 Work-oriented: Support for Self-Reliance Scheme (SSR), emphasizing employment incentives, reducing dependency.
  • 有條件援助：居港年期、工作要求。 Conditional Support: Residency years, work requirements.

影響分析 Impact Analysis

批評：忽略結構性貧窮，加劇不平等 (連結Lecture 2福利權利vs.責任辯論)。 Criticism: Overlooks structural poverty, exacerbates inequality (linking Lecture 2 rights vs. responsibilities debate).

Features like small government and self-reliance are directly embodied in CSSA's residual model. This makes assistance short-term relief rather than long-term poverty reduction, criticizing it for exacerbating inequality and stigmatization. CSSA定位分析 CSSA Positioning Analysis

  • 傾向Workfare：手段測試嚴格、SSR計劃、「較少資格」原則。 Leans toward Workfare: Strict means-testing, SSR scheme, "less eligibility" principle.
  • 部分Activation：就業培訓、有條件收入補助。 Partial Activation: Employment training, conditional income supplements.
  • 缺乏Entitlement：非無條件權利、高門檻。 Lacks Entitlement: Not unconditional rights, high thresholds.

權利 vs. 責任辯論 Rights vs. Responsibilities Debate

CSSA視福利為責任多於權利，傾向Workfare減少依賴 (Lecture 2)。 CSSA views welfare more as responsibility than right, leaning toward Workfare to reduce dependency (Lecture 2).

Policy Review of CSSA: Rights and Responsibilities under Neoliberalism

Slide 2-3: Introduction (2 min)

  • CSSA Background and Importance: Residual safety net, overlooking structural poverty.
  • Policy Goals and Challenges: Reduce dependency but exacerbate inequality.

  • Neoliberalism Core Features: Small government, individual self-reliance.
  • CSSA Embodiment: Means-testing, work-oriented, conditional aid.
  • Impact Analysis: Criticism for exacerbating inequality.

  • CSSA Positioning: Leans toward Workfare, partial Activation, lacks Entitlement.
  • Rights vs. Responsibilities Debate.

  • Procedural Justice: Partially met but with bias and low representativeness.
  • Care: Ignores care needs.
  • Honesty: Trust deficit, high non-take-up rates.
